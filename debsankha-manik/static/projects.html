    Title: A study of photoelectric effect with variable intensity light source
    Here we performed the regular photoelectric effect experiment while varying the source intensity. Contrary to the quantum-mechanical prediction, a dip in the stopping potential was observed. By means of a seperate experiment, we proved that the stopping potential didn't actually decrease, but the time taken by the circuit to reach equillibrium got so very long.

  • expand Summer 2009

    Title: Simulation of 2-D Ising spin systems in the Monte Carlo method
    The code to simulate a 2-D Ising spin system in the Monte Carlo method was written in python, using the Metropolis algorithm. The change in the net magnetization of the system was monitored for different temperatures, at first without any external magnetic field and then with some nonzero external magnetic field. Surprisingly I found the net magnetization to increase initially with increase in temperature. This behaviour continued right uptil the phase transition. Intrigued, I added some code to my program to visualize the system (Using VPython). And then I found that for low temperatures, the system is frequently getting stuck at configurations like ribbons: a continuous strip of up spins flanked on both sides by strips of down spins or vice versa. We could not find any problem with my code. Therefore we concluded this to be a drawback of the Metropolis algorithm.
    Instructor: Dr. P. A. Sreeram Report Codes

    Biology

    Title: A statistical survey on the similarities between the fingerprints in populations of genetically related people.
    We took the fingerprints of people from quite a few families. Then we measured the degree of similarity between the fingerprints of genetically related people by means of image prossesing (done with the handy Python Imaging Library). We also got the degree of similarity for people who are not genetically related. By comparing those two distributions, we concluded that genetic relatedness does not ensure any similarity between the fingerprints of two human beings.
    Partner: Arobendo Mondal , Instructor: Dr. Sankar Maity

    Physics

    Title: Investigating the normal modes of an unsymmetrical coupled pendulum.
    We monitored the motion of a bob of an unsymmetrical coupled pendulum wrapped with retro-reflective foil using a CCD camera. We determined the normal modes by a Fast Fourier Transform of the displacement Vs. time graph. We studied the change of those frequencies with the change in the mass of the bob and matched with the theoretically derived formula.
    Partner: Parijat Sarkar , Instructor: Dr. Gautam Deb Mukherjee.
